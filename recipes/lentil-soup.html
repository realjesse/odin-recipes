Lentil Soup

Description:

Through the annals of time, lentil soup has bridged ancient civilizations, uniting cultures with its heartening simplicity. From the Mesopotamian plains to India's verdant fields, its journey is a fragrant testament to human resilience and culinary creativity. Today, each bowl is a delicious reminder of our shared heritage and the beauty of humble ingredients.

The following is taken from Akis Petretzikis superb recipe Greek Lentil Soup, I highly recommend trying some of his other recipes out, as they are all of great quality.

Ingredients:

  • 250 g lentils
  • 2 tablespoon(s) olive oil
  • 1 onion, medium
  • 2 carrots
  • 2 stick(s) celery
  • 2 clove(s) of garlic
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon(s) cumin
  • 1 pinch chili flakes
  • 400 g canned tomatoes
  • 1 vegetable bouillon cube
  • 1 1/2 liter water
  • 1 teaspoon(s) salt

Steps:

  1. Rinse the lentils and drain.
  2. Place a large pot over medium heat.
  3. Coarsely chop the onion and add them to the pot along with the olive oil.
  4. Chop the carrots and celery into small slices.
  5. Add them to the pot and saute for 3-5 minutes, until they soften.
  6. Thinly slice the garlic and add to the pot along with the bay leaves, chili flakes and cumin.
  7. Stir and add the lentils, chopped tomatoes and vegetable stock.
  8. Cover the pot and simmer for 45 minutes over medium to low heat.
  9. When ready, add the salt.
