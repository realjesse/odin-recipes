Mushroom Risotto

Description:

Amid the rolling fields of Italy, where the soft hum of rice paddies meets the forest's whisper, mushroom risotto took root, a dance of earth's finest ingredients. Embracing the creamy alchemy of arborio rice and the woodsy aroma of wild fungi, it became a testament to culinary artistry, tradition, and the patience of stirring hands. Today, its velvety texture and rich flavors bring warmth to tables worldwide, celebrating the essence of simplicity and the soul of Italian cuisine.

The following recipe is taken from Billy Parisi's Best Creamy Risotto Recipe. He is absolutely one of my favorite chef's and I recommend anyone who is new to intermediate in cooking to check him out! Careful though, this makes a lot! Consider sizing down.

Ingredients:

  • 1-2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 peeled small diced yellow onion
  • 3 finely minced garlic cloves
  • 4 cups arborio rice
  • 1 cup chardonnay
  • 10 cups chicken stock
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup grated parmesan cheese
  • To taste sea salt and pepper

Steps:

  1. Add the olive oil to a large pot or rondeau over medium-low heat
  2. Pour in the onions and cook for 8-10 minutes or until lightly browned and translucent
  3. Add in the garlic and cook for 1 to 2 minutes
  4. Next pour in the rice and toast for 3 to 4 minutes while stirring every 30 seconds
  5. Deglaze with the wine and stir constantly until all of it has been absorbed into the rice
  6. Add 4-8 ounces of warm chicken stock at a time to the rice while constantly stirring until it has been absorbed and then repeat the process until the arborio rice is cooked and al dente, or slightly crunchy
  7. Stir in the cream, parmesan cheese, salt and pepper and cook for 2 to 3 more minutes
  8. Serve with more parmesan cheese, pepper and optional parsley garnish
